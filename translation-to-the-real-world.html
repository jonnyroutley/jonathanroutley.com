How could we make Spider-Man's webs?

In the last section we determined that the UTS (ultimate tensile strength) of Spider-Man's webs was about 5000Mpa - 5x the strength of 'average' spider silk and 10x as strong as structural steel. We (the real world), however, has never managed to properly reproduce regular spider silk artifically, let alone super strength silk. Spider silk has long been coveted due to its impressive properties - it has an extraordinary strength:weight ratio (6x that of steel), is highly recyclable/biodregradable (some spiders eat their webs and are able to recycle it with up to 90% efficiency).
